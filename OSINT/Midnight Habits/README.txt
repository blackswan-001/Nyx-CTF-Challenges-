====================================================
  Midnight Habits
  Category : OSINT   |   Difficulty : Medium
====================================================

  "We didn't break in. He left the door open."
                              — leaked attacker communiqué

====================================================

Three weeks ago, Nyx Research suffered a targeted
intrusion. Internal systems were accessed. Sensitive
archives were exfiltrated. The breach lasted 4 hours
before NIGHTWATCH flagged the anomaly.

The attackers have since gone public.

In a post on a known threat actor forum, they claimed
the intrusion required no zero-days, no exploits, and
no sophisticated tooling. They said it took one person,
two hours, and an open browser.

They published a breakdown of their alleged method —
screenshots of four pages they claim were publicly
accessible and used to reconstruct a staff member's
login credentials.

The claim: they profiled an employee, pieced together
his password from his own digital footprint, and
walked straight through the front door.

Nyx Research's incident response team is not convinced.
The published screenshots could be fabricated.
The claimed path could be a distraction.

That's where you come in.

----------------------------------------------------
  YOUR MISSION
----------------------------------------------------

The attackers have provided four pages as evidence
of their method. You have been given access to the
same pages as part of the investigation.

Verify the attacker's claim.

If their stated path is legitimate — that a staff
member's credentials could be reconstructed purely
from publicly visible information across these pages
— then the intrusion vector is confirmed.

If the path is fabricated or incomplete, the
investigation takes a different direction entirely.

There is one way to know for certain:

Walk the path yourself.
Find the email.
Reconstruct the password.
Attempt the login.

If you get in, the breach was real.
The flag is your confirmation.

----------------------------------------------------
  THE ATTACKER'S CLAIM
----------------------------------------------------

"The target was careless. Everything we needed was
already out there — his forum profile, a complaint
he filed, his social media. He practically wrote
his own password in public. We just read it."

Four pages were leaked as evidence:

  [ forum.html       ]  Internal staff forum
  [ complaint.html   ]  Delivery complaint record
  [ social.html      ]  Social media profile
  [ portal.html      ]  Staff access portal

----------------------------------------------------
  HINTS  (read only as many as you need)
----------------------------------------------------

Hint 1 — "The attacker said it took two hours.
          It shouldn't take you that long."

Hint 2 — "Read everything. Not just the posts —
          the comments, the dates, the details."

Hint 3 — "The password wasn't hidden.
          It was just never meant to be assembled."

----------------------------------------------------
  TOOLS YOU MAY FIND USEFUL
----------------------------------------------------

  A browser, a notepad, and attention to detail.

  The attack surface is human, not technical.
